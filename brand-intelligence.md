## Logo-use decision
The official Webflow CDN exposes the mark, but the available build environment could not retrieve the SVG bytes through its network boundary and the GitHub connector does not accept non-GitHub binary URLs. To avoid tracing, redrawing, modifying, or hotlinking the mark, the campaign uses a clearly typeset `1mind` employer identifier above the fold with an immediate independent-candidate qualifier. This is a documented technical incompatibility, not a claim that a typeset word is the official logo. No candidate-created mark substitutes for the company identity.

## Typography evidence and decision
The public website presents oversized, tightly tracked, heavy geometric/grotesk display typography with clean sans-serif supporting copy. No authoritative public font specification or downloadable licensed font package was located in the retrieved sources. The campaign uses installed `Inter Display` for the structural display character and `Lato` for body copy. These are licensed local substitutes; neither is represented as 1mind's official typeface.

## Photography, product, and interface character
The official site is highly product- and character-led, with bold editorial type, dark/light contrast, rounded portrait and interface forms, saturated purple, and a playful but precise AI-human tone. Because no official photography or interface asset was necessary to communicate this internal operations thesis, the campaign uses original code-native circuit and memory visuals derived from the role, not copied product UI.

## Geometry and motion
- Circular memory motif acknowledging the visual weight of the official circular mark without tracing it.
- Rounded nodes, strong editorial scale, high black/white contrast, and controlled purple glow.
- Motion follows a complete closed circuit so work signal, boundary, build, adoption, and returned evidence remain causally legible.

## Recommended use by artifact
- Website: fullest black/purple/lavender system, animated memory circuit, candidate-original lime evidence trace.
- Resume and cover letter: white executive documents, restrained purple rule and identity text, no fake corporate letterhead.
- Brief, plan, and review: same typography, rules, lavender highlights, and dark memory cells.
