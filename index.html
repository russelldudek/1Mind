The real mandate is not “more AI.” It is a company that learns how to operate with AI.

1mind is defining Autonomous Customer Experience externally. Internally, the AI Operations Lead must convert scattered opportunity into a disciplined learning system—starting in Sales, then compounding across the business.

Measure whether the work changed—not whether an automation exists.

These are measurement categories for discovery, not asserted 1mind baselines or targets.

Business outcome

Cycle time, first-pass quality, conversion-supporting behavior, rework, or another workflow-specific unit.

Does this change the result that justified the work?

Retained adoption

Repeated use inside the real workflow, not demo attendance or initial activation.

Did the mechanism become a habit with an accountable owner?

Trust + control

Override patterns, exception closure, missing context, approval boundaries, and failure visibility.

Can people use it confidently without surrendering judgment?

Economics

Labor hours avoided, rework reduced, maintenance burden, model/tool cost, and engineering support.

Is the durable benefit worth the total operating cost?

Reuse + learning

Context patterns, prompts, guardrails, components, training, and evidence that improve the next workflow.

Did this release leave the company smarter?

First 90 days: earn the right to scale.

Begin with Sales because the role says to—but design the learning so it can travel.

Days 1–30

Read the work

  • Map priority Sales workflows, decisions, handoffs, systems, and existing AI use.
  • Create an opportunity register with value, readiness, authority, adoption, and ownership fields.
  • Baseline two bounded workflows and establish lightweight guardrails with RevOps and Engineering.
Days 31–60

Prove in the workflow

  • Prototype two interventions with explicit human authority and failure modes.
  • Run live-work pilots, instrument retained use and workflow outcomes, and document overrides.
  • Train the workflow owner and users inside the operating cadence—not as a separate AI event.
Days 61–90

Compound the memory

  • Harden one proven pattern; hold, revise, or stop the other based on evidence.
  • Publish reusable context, guardrail, ownership, training, and measurement assets.
  • Launch a monthly operating-memory review and select the next adjacent GTM workflow.

The strongest reasonable objection

“Russell has senior operations titles. Will he actually build, iterate, and live in the workflow?”

That is the right question.

My through-line is mechanism-building: observe work, make friction visible, prototype the intervention, connect technical and business owners, teach the new standard, instrument the result, and iterate. I currently work directly across operational systems and AI-assisted workflows at Vape-Jet, and I use Python, APIs, LLM patterns, RAG, human-in-the-loop design, and automation tools to bring ideas to life.

I would not claim deep production platform engineering or enterprise RevOps administration where the evidence does not support it. My value is knowing what must be true in the work, building the bounded proof, and partnering with Engineering when the solution becomes durable infrastructure.

Questions I would bring to the room

Where has internal AI already created real pull—and where has it created rework, mistrust, or hidden maintenance?

Which Sales decision or handoff is currently expensive enough to justify changing the workflow, not merely adding a tool?

What should this role own directly, and what must remain with Revenue Operations, Engineering, Security, or the workflow owner?

How will 1mind distinguish an exciting prototype from a pattern that deserves company-wide reuse?

What would make the hiring team say after six months: “we now operate differently because this role exists”?
