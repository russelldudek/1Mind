Nina Taylor and the 1mind hiring team

1mind is asking a deceptively hard question: what does an AI-native company look like from the inside out? The answer is not a larger collection of automations. It is an operating system that converts workflow friction into bounded solutions, retained adoption, measurable outcomes, and reusable learning.

That is the work I have spent my career doing at the intersection of operations and technology. At Vape-Jet, I work directly across production, purchasing, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. The job is not to decorate operations with AI. It is to make signals visible, clarify ownership, improve handoffs, build mechanisms people will use, and hold the system accountable to better work.

Through DudeWorth, I develop AI-readiness and opportunity frameworks that evaluate value, feasibility, data readiness, governance, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value. I also design agentic workflow patterns that make context, human judgment, escalation, evaluation, and governance explicit. Earlier, at Amazon, I built operating routines, standard work, throughput visibility, and escalation mechanisms in a 24/7 customer-backward environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. At Compunetics, I translated between engineering, manufacturing, quality, customers, and high-reliability delivery.

My candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/1Mind/ shows how I would apply that experience to 1mind. The central artifact—AI Operating Memory—starts with a real Sales workflow, bounds the AI role and human authority, builds the smallest useful intervention, installs adoption inside the work, and returns evidence as a reusable operating asset. The accompanying 90-day plan and review mechanism show how I would partner with Revenue Operations first and then scale proven patterns across GTM and the broader company.

The strongest reasonable concern is that my recent title is Director of Operations rather than a traditional AI Operations or RevOps engineering title. I would not blur that boundary. My fit is the operator-builder combination the role describes: strategic judgment about where AI belongs, enough Python/API and workflow fluency to prototype and connect systems, the discipline to bring Engineering in when solutions require production ownership, and the change leadership to make the new mechanism trusted and durable.

1mind’s external promise is one continuous memory across the buyer journey. I would be excited to help build the internal equivalent—so every successful workflow leaves the company smarter, faster, and more capable than before.

Carpe diem,
